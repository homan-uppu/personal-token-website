Albert Einstein - German-born physicist who revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and the universe with his theory of relativity.
Marie Curie - Polish-French physicist and chemist who pioneered research on radioactivity and discovered polonium and radium.
Rumi - 13th-century Persian poet and Sufi mystic whose poetry about love and spirituality transcends cultures.
Ada Lovelace - British mathematician who wrote the first computer program, recognizing the potential of computational machines.
Srinivasa Ramanujan - Indian mathematician who made extraordinary contributions to mathematical analysis and number theory.
Tu Youyou - Chinese pharmaceutical chemist who discovered artemisinin to treat malaria, saving millions of lives.
Emmy Noether - German mathematician who revolutionized abstract algebra and theoretical physics with her theorem connecting symmetry and conservation laws.
Leonardo Da Vinci - Italian Renaissance polymath whose work spanned art, science, engineering, and anatomy.
Galileo - Italian astronomer and physicist who improved the telescope and supported heliocentrism.
Aryabhatta - 5th-century Indian mathematician and astronomer who introduced the concept of zero and calculated pi.
Nikola Tesla - Serbian-American inventor who developed the alternating current electrical system.
Grace Hopper - American computer scientist who invented the first compiler and pioneered computer programming.
Al-Khwarizmi - Persian mathematician who founded algebra and introduced Indian numerals to the Western world.
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart - Austrian composer whose musical genius produced over 600 works.
Rabindranath Tagore - Bengali polymath who reshaped literature and music in South Asia.
William Shakespeare - English playwright and poet whose works profoundly influenced literature.
